Session Overview

We spent the night in Tinkford. When we arose the next morning, we ate a very interesting breakfast or mechanically manufactured food ranging in degrees of cooked. We then consulted with Gnome Mommy to get the location of the Cave of Knowledge. She marked our map and bade us good luck. She also gave us a note to give to the guards so they would know that we were there with her permission

Following some very un-eventful travel to the east, we arrived a the cave. After handing the note to the guards and trying to get any information out of them we could, we dove in. We did bet the guards that we would be able to go in and come back out in 10 minutes but luckily they forgot that.

After following the main tunnel a decent ways we came upon a hallway that had some suits of armor in it and a door at the far end. Written above the door was a puzzle that involved the door saying numbers and then people saying numbers back. I know that I am a very intelligent wizard but for some reason the puzzle eluded me. Luckily William was with us and as if by divine inspiration was able to tell us the answer, something to do with spelling and numbers, i don't really know.

Following this door we entered into a large circular room. On the lefthand wall, there was a large hour glass mounted to the wall on a poll that spun. To the right of the door as we entered was a pool of water and across from us there was another door, closed, with a pile of rocks to the left of it and a chest to the right. Gregor had not taken a bath in some time so he decided to check out the pool while I went and looked at the hourglass. As I began to inspect it, the door we entered through swung closed and the hourglass flipped itself and began pouring sand. A this point i assumed the room to be a trap and so I fired off a cursory Ray of Frost at the chest to ensure that is was not a mimic as I have come to understand they love places like this. I think Alice may have misunderstood my warning shot because as soon as the chest didn't move I knew it was not such a beast but she continued to wail on it the whole time. As the hour glass emptied for the first time, the pile of rocks beside the door formed up into an Earth Elemental and began to attack. Knowing as usual that it was up to me to keep my party alive, I cast Hold Person on the monstrosity to hold it in place while they beat it up. Through this the hourglass continued to drain and as it emptied it flipped itself over and started again. About that time I believe Alice took the final swing on the rocks, which was fortunate, because the puddle Gregor had been bathing in, turned into a Water Elemental and decided to come after me for some reason. Using another well put Hold Person I grabbed this monster as well and prevented it from even getting an attack off on us. I then used my incredible strength to try and hold the hour glass in place but did drop it for a second as we fought the elementa, which was lucky because the third emptying opened the door on our way out. Walking up to the door we finally got to inspect Alice's handiwork and the chest and found some, thankfully unbroken, healing potions.

Recap of previous session

Summary:: In this session, the group journeyed to the village of Grethwyl, discovering it in a state of decay and realizing they needed to act quickly. After navigating through a maze of plant walls, they confronted and ultimately defeated a dragon named Oakshade, who had taken on the role of the Thorn Mother by killing the real one. Following Oakshade's defeat, Fenna chose not to assume the position of Thorn Mother herself, opting for a council-based leadership instead. The session concluded with celebrations in the village, though one party member felt frustrated by their lack of recognition and support from a deity figure.

Session Overview

We awoke the next morning in a new Grethwyl, the people immediately trying to reclaim their town and lives much like the forest around them trying to reclaim it's own loss. Celebration still filled the air if in a more muted tone. There was no disdain for the work that these people had to undertake, just a pleasure at it being work they chose.

Once we had all risen from a well deserved night, I don't think Fenna realizes how much work she does for all of us and finds it hard to relax and was of course the first one up. Thaynera greeted us as we arose and let us know she would hasten the next part of our journey once we were prepared to leave. Fenna said her fairwells to her family and the town she was once destined to lead. (I can't say that I understand relenting a position like that. While a different kind of power, leadership brings with it it's own strengths. I do suppose it bring its own restrictions as well though.) With bags packed and farewells given we approached Thaynera. She thanked us once more for our help and then transported us back to ReRe's Cave. I do not know what type of spell this was but I imagine the weaving is similar to the portals Thaldor has set up, I will have to ask him if he has seen it before.

We had brief but not brief enough interaction with ReRe where, for reasons I can't understand, Fenna tried to get her to buy jerky? I need to keep an eye on Fenna, loosing responsibility seems to have affected her in ways we didn't consider. Jumping through the portal back to Cinderwind College was uneventful if not uninteresting. We did have to wait a good 10 minutes for Thaldor to finally come let us out. He arrived with some urgency, beckoned us to follow him to the cafeteria, and then ran away. Assumedly he ran the whole way because he was not in sight once we exited the offices.

When we reached the Cafeteria we decided to make a show of entering as we had just returned from killing a dragon, no small feat. We slammed the doors open and made the wind whip around us as well as some lights shine from behind. The entrance was grand if I do say so myself, however it was not the time. The cafeteria had been comletely changed, hanging from the ceiling were banners and decorations, all the tables had been moved to the walls and set up in little stations with some magic gadgets on them. A podium was set up at the far end of the hall where Thaldor was apparently giving a speech. It took me a second to catch up but apparently this was the opening ceremony for some annual competition between us and the other magic school Seiluna Institute.

Thaldor welcomed the other students and headmaster and explained the games to us. Using some new illusion magic technology, 3 teams of 4 students from each school would be competing in an illusory competition. I believe this was to make the event safer as apparently in past years.... events ocurred. I also believe though, knowing that there were no consequenses ultimately made the events more dangerous but that is not a complaint. The final team standing would be awarded 5,000gp as well as bragging rights for the rest of the year. It did seem like we kicked out a group of 3rd years that were supposed to compete but I will let them sort that with Thaldor.

With that explained, all teams were sent to one of the tables against the walls and handed a fancy circlet to put on.

This magic was truly stunning. Upon putting the circlet on my head all of my senses seemed to be captured and put under the control of this device. I still felt like me and looked like me but I was no longer in the cafeteria. Around me was a blank room with nothing to distinguish it, my fellows were there with me and seemed to be adjusting to this magic as well. Locked to the corners of my vision were different pieces of information, apparently the method of tracking information within the game. It was a very creative application of illusion magic that I will have to see if I can replicate. At the top of my vision a large box appeared, within it were the words "Enter the door" and an empty box. At the same time, on the wall across from us a door appeared. We shared a look between us, confirming that everyone saw this, and then walked up and opened the door. The box was filled with a check mark and we walked into the next room. Assumedly this was just to get us used to the device and how it worked and not actually a challenge (although there is rumor that one of the Seiluna Institute teams didn't actually make it this far.)

On the other side of the door was a long hallway decorated in a similar fashion with torches lining the walls every 25 feet or so. The only noticable difference was the floor, aside from directly where we entered and the far end of the hall, the entire floor was covered in mud. As we all stepped into the room the door behind us didn't close, it disappeared completely. As the door vanished, the quest description changed as well; "Reach the other end.". We all assumed this would be a fairly simple task, and in hindsight we should have found this suspicious but I believe we still just assumed it was trying to acclimate us to the magic.

I took the first steps into the mud with Orianna trudging close behind me. I made it about 15' or so before Alice and Fenna followed, walking about 15' behind and to the side of us. As I went to take my next step all of the torched on the walls fell from their sconces as one landing in the mud. Now, I imagine the mud was some sort of magic concoction, seeing that the mud immediately ignited and started spreading. Suddenly this challenge made way more sense. Realizing that we needed to get through this and trying to prevent our way out from being closed off I sent a blast of cold at one of the fires as soon as it caught at the end of the hall to prevent it spreading. I took a couple more steps and the situation became even more dire. I placed my foot on the ground and heard a loud metallic click followed by an odd pain in my leg. Looking down I found my leg caught between the jaws of a large bear trap. The game must have been doing something to my pain senses as I felt it and knew it had happened but it was not near the pain it should have been. In order to escape the trap as well as progress further towards our goal I used the new Misty Step spell that I discovered.

Looking back I should have more effectively communicated my plans to the team as I believe they thought I was trying to leave them behind. I thought that maybe there would be a switch or method of extinguishing the flames at the far end so I figured that if I could get there quickly I would be able to save them from the flames. This was not the case. While not too dangerous in reality this did take some time to get through. Fenna and Alice stuck together through most of it, at one point using one of Alice's hand axes and rope to make a type of rope swing to avoid the flaming mud. Orianna took a different approach and decided that summoning a boar from her bag-of-tricks was a good idea. I don't know that I will ever understand that woman but I do applaud her creativity, now to find a way to channel it.

We made it through, the rest of it was the same story again. We waded through the mud, got caught in a number of traps, and I think Alice and Fenna both got a bit more singed than they would have liked but we made it to the end.

4 teams made it through this challenge, and I don't envy the ones that didn't for multiple reasons, What a way to go, even in an illusion.

Summary

Summary:: The group awoke in the town of Grethwyl, where they witnessed the townspeople beginning to reclaim their lives after recent events. After saying their farewells, they were transported back to ReRe's Cave and then to Cinderwind College, where they discovered an annual competition with Seiluna Institute was about to begin. Using new illusion magic, teams were challenged to navigate through a series of rooms, starting with a hallway covered in magical mud that ignited when touched. The team faced various traps and obstacles but ultimately managed to reach the end of the challenge, alongside three other teams.

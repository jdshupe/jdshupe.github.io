Summary

Summary:: In this session, the group journeyed to the village of Grethwyl, discovering it in a state of decay and realizing they needed to act quickly. After navigating through a maze of plant walls, they confronted and ultimately defeated a dragon named Oakshade, who had taken on the role of the Thorn Mother by killing the real one. Following Oakshade's defeat, Fenna chose not to assume the position of Thorn Mother herself, opting for a council-based leadership instead. The session concluded with celebrations in the village, though one party member felt frustrated by their lack of recognition and support from a deity figure.

Session Overview

We rose early the next morning to make the journey north to Grethwyl. This leg of the journey was far easier than the one to get here since Fenna and William are familiar with this region of the woods. Over the course of our travels the last couple days I have come to realize I am very luck to have survived my first trip into these woods. I knew Jakunta to be a formidable foe but did not realize quite how dangerous these woods can be.

The trip was uneventful until we started nearing our destination. I had previously asked Fenna and William to let me know when we were approaching a mile or so out from town as I would then be able to scope out the approaching trials for us. Fortunately or unfortunately I should not have worried about asking for as we neared that marker the forest changed drastically. Ahead of us we saw a solid wall of vines and forest growth roughly 20 feet tall with a green haze/fog right at the top. We paused for a moment for me to cast clairvoyance and figure out what awaited us in the village. Using the descriptions give to me by Fenna and William, I was easily able to see into their town. Around myself I saw the village as normal, except for no animals around. However, as I maintained the spell, the images started to flicker back and forth between what was described to me and what can only be called a disaster. The house were nearly destroyed, all the plant were dead, there were no animals in sight. Lined up at a pyre in the center of town seemed to be all of the townsfolk and they were carrying seemingly random objects. Ending the spell I communicated both visions of the town without much certainty as to what we would find ahead but certain that we should hurry.

Realizing we needed to get there quickly I recommended we send Gregor over the wall to see if he could see anything. I began to just send him on his way when Orianna recommended we tie him to her arrow and shoot him onto the wall. With much reluctance and a promise of treats I was able to convince him to do so. Orianna took aim, loosed, and hit exactly at the rim of the wall. Gregor then reached up to pull himself over the lip of the wall, but when his hand touched the fog he broke our bond and went back to the fae wild. Assumedly the fog harmed him and rather than die, he shifted planes. What a bother, he was already upset at being shot through the air and now I have to deal with this.

Seeing as we couldn't go over the wall and we didn't know what was beyond it, I did what I thought was rational; I used my new fancy cross bow to shoot a hole in it. It was after all made up of plants. Fenna quickly tried to yell at me but I reminded her that she had said burning things and swearing were both allowed now that we were on our way to eliminate the imposter. Through the hole in the wall we saw yet another wall of plants. I thought maybe there was a platform over it and this was an interior passage for a fortification so I shot through the next wall as well. Lo and behold, another wall! We all looked at it for a couple seconds before Alice and I both had the same revelation, we were looking at the interior walls of a maze! It was in this moment that I grew a larger appreciation for my muscle bound companion despite her young years and lacking intellect, maybe I should try to help her more. Regardless, knowing that there was a maze of unknown size ahead of us, I recommended we continue to blast our way through as it had worked thus far.

The excursion through the maze was relatively uneventful. We encountered a couple wolves that Alice and William dispatched with ease, followed by some other things that I frankly don't remember. One that did impress me though was, as I shot through like the 20th wall, a wave of poisonous gas like what killed Gregor, came spilling out. In a moment of pure instinct, Fenna conjured and absolutely amazing Wind Wall and kept the gas contained. It seemed like she cast the spell before she even knew it herself. I must have more respect for my allies as they are growing as much as I am through all of this.

Some time later (it may have been faster to do the maze but we will never know I guess) I fired through the last wall and Grethwyl lay before us. The village matched up with the decayed/destroyed images that I was seeing earlier, and as I reflect I can only assume that maybe that was the result of what I was looking for and the reality of the location being so different, I will have to study this. Something I missed in my earlier survey was a large throne at the center of town and sitting upon it was Oakshade. In retrospect, blasting through the walls may not have been the most subtle of ways of entering the town but it definitely got the job done. The entire town turned to look at us and witness as Fenna absolutely bombed being subtle. Oakshade did some rambling and monologueing about "why I should rule" and "why you suck" among other things but ultimately common ground could not be found.

The fight started with Orianna throwing a tiger?? I don't fully understand how her mind works ever but I do envy her attitude. I tried to take some of Oakshade's companions out of the fight with a slow spell but it only got 2 of them. Rather quickly we took out her 4 body guards (I found out their names to be Brad, Chad, Gallad, and Thad; apparenlty Fenna dated all of them, she is not who I thought) and were left with Oakshade herself. Just as it looked like we might finish the job her body began to convulse and writhe. Wings began to push through and rip her back as all parts of her started to grow, swell, and darken. Turns out she's a dragon (or should I say was ;)). She ranted on again about "youngest sibling of 13" and "no respect" and "unable to make a lair" all kinds of garbage. Essentially, she wasn't strong enough to make her own way in the world so she figured, why not take someone elses work and put her claws on it. She found the actuall Thorn Mother Oakshade in the woods one day and took her captive, learning enough about her to be able to play the part well. She then killed her and came back to the village. Unfortunately for our friend Fenna this was enough time for her to go through the ritual and be picked as the replacement Thorn Mother, creating all the confusion.

Well, we killed her. It wasn't easy, well maybe for me, but it was a good fight. At one point Orianna tried to convince get-dragon-name to become president of the Hammerhead Isles, again, what is going on in her brain. That didn't work though so we just offed her. All in a days work for us honestly. I am a bit disappointed, most of my friends were unconsious at the end of the fight and I had finally come up with a stellar plan to save the day when the freaking towns people shot her out of the sky. Bit of a let down if i'm honest but we will see if I have another chance to do it.

Everything after the fight is a bit of a blur honestly. I know the towns people celebrated the return of Fenna after realizing that she was the rightful Thorn Mother. Thaynera came out of the woods at some point and let everyone know what had happened alongside Fenna. Fenna also decided that she no longer wanted to be the Thorn Mother. I don't know if this is because she decided that travel suits her or that she doesn't like the idea of leading or that ultimately all leadership leads to corruption regardless of how pure your motives may be. No matter the reason, her and the Thorn Mother decided that the people would be better led by a group of people so that no one voice carried to much sway in what happened in the town. I do think this was a good idea.

Celebration ensued and you might guess. I did find a time to go talk with Thaynera. I explained to her what I have observed, that Fenna carries a heavy burden in our group. That of keeping us alive. I think she enjoys doing it but I know that being the only one to do so is a lot. I asked Thaynera if she might be able to teach me or to help lighten the burden on Fenna in some way. In response, she said that "Healing is a magic I can not give to you but one that you must find yourself" or some bull crap like that. Honestly, gods still suck. Here I am, being very genuine and trying to help her follower, and all I get is some sage mumbo jumbo about "find yourself young one". I obviously did not tell her any of this but instead thanked her for her help and went to enjoy the night.

I did not resummon Gregor. I can't deal with the drama right now.
